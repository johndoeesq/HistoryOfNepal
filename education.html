SILGADI FAREWELL

Doti, 1966. Villagers from Silgadi in farwest Nepal bidding farewell to women travelling to Kathmandu to pursue higher education. The journey of 800km back then took nearly two weeks to complete. At that time ,education for women was not as easy to men.Women were not encouraged or allowed to go to school even then because of the traditional nature of society. It was the huge movement happened back then which was the start of great revolution in the history of education for women.

DURBAR HIGH SCHOOL

Durbar High School which translates to Palace High School, opened in 1892 is the oldest modern school in Nepal. Located in Ranipokhari, Kathmandu,it originally taught only members of the ruling family, but was opened to private citizens in 1902.The founder of the Rana regime, Jung Bahadur Kunwar, later known as Jung Bahadur Rana, decided to give his children an English education rather than the traditional religiously oriented training.Because of his embarrasement at UK of not being able to speak English while told to deliver something ,he decided to teach his children and also said that "aru kura na sikau tara afno chora lai angrezi bolna sikau" which translated to "even if you don't teach your son anything , teach your son english". So he hired an English tutor back then in 1854 to teach his son Englsih language.The trend expanded to Durbar High school making English a prestigious language back then to till now.

Some parts of the school were destroyed in the April 2015 Nepal earthquake.

GURUKULA

This is a makeshift(Gurukula) classroom in Okhaldhunga in 1967 AD. “Gurukula” is the education system where “Guru” (which means a teacher in Sanskrit) teaches his “Sikshyas” (students) collectively (Kula in Sanskrit means an extended family) under a particular setting regardless of one’s caste, creed, or social standing.At this time, there were 300 schools and about 10,000 students in the country with a literacy rate of 5%. While a very few people from different backgrounds could enjoy the benefits of education, women were far away from the enlightment.

TRI CHANDRA COLLEGE

Chandra Shumsher, the Rana Prime Minister, established Tribhuvan Chandra Intermediate College now named as Tri-Chandra College in 1918.The establishment of Tri-Chandra College built the base for higher education in the country.Situated at the heart of Kathmandu is the eldest academic institution of Nepal in the context of higher education. It was established in 1916 A.D.(1975 B.S.) and is popular in society as "Tri-Chandra College".Tri-Chandra Campus isrenowned since beginning not only within the country but outside too. It has adequately contributed to the nation through the production of skillful manpower.

SCHOOL LEAVING CERTIFICATE

The School Leaving Certificate (SLC) examination was initially introduced in Nepal in 1934 and the Durbar High School which was run exclusively to educated the children of the judgment authoritarian Rana dynasty in Nepal became the very first school to take part in the SLC tests. In 1880, after the Durbar High School managed making an offer with the University of Calcutta in Kolkata, India, the very first batch of Nepali students went to appear in SLC-eqivalent entryway tests in the university. The government bore all the expenses of the students’ travel and accommodation and Dhir Shamsher’s 2nd kid Khanga Shamsher was among the examinees to attend the examinations. Khanga Shamsher could not pass the exams, but received a medal anyway due to Check SLC Result that he was a student from the Rana household. Dhir Shamsher’s another boy, Chandra Shamsher, was the very first Nepali to pass the SLC-equivalent entryway examinations of the Calcutta University.

Since from 2072 BS, SLC is transformed into GCSE which lead to grades as results making SLC to be a historical IRON GATE for the student who faced it.

PUBLIC SERVICE EXAM

The Public Service Commission (Nepali: लोक सेवा आयोग) of Nepal was established on 15 June 1951. It is the main constitutional body involved in selecting meritorious candidates required by Government of Nepal for Civil Service Vacancy.It was and is regarded as one of the most credible modes of recruitment. Back then in Nepal,people having government jobs were credited as the richer of all and wisest of many. It was easy back then to have a girl form richest family if a person has equipied with the sword of government job.
